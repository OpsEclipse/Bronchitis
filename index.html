What is Bronchitis

Bronchitis is a respiratory system disease. Bronchitis is the inflammation of the air passages between your nose and lungs. including the windpipe or trachea and the larger air tubes of the lung that bring air from the trachea(bronchial)

Types of Bronchitis

There are different types of Bronchitis that all have different effect on your body. Some cannot be cured and some can be.

Acute

Acute Bronchitis is usually cause by a viral infection, but can also be caused by a bacterial infection. Acute Bronchitis can be cured without much complications.

Chronic

Chronic bronchitis is a sign of serious lung disease that may be slowed but cannot be cured.

Causes

What are some of the cause of acute Bronchitis and Chronic Bronchitis.

Causes Viral and bacterial infections Environmental factors if you inhale to much smoke, smog, or chemical fumes. Other lung conditions Asthma

Symptoms

Symptoms For either acute bronchitis or chronic bronchitis: signs and symptoms may include: Cough Production of mucus (sputum), which can be clear, white, yellowish-gray or green in color — rarely, it may be streaked with blood Fatigue Shortness of breath Slight fever and chills Chest discomfort If you have acute bronchitis, you might have cold symptoms, such as a mild headache or body aches. Chronic bronchitis is defined as a productive cough that lasts at least three months, with recurring bouts occurring for at least two consecutive years.

Treatment

In some circumstances, your doctor may recommend other medications, including: Cough medicine. If your cough keeps you from sleeping, you might try cough suppressants at bedtime. Other medications. If you have allergies, asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), your doctor may recommend an inhaler and other medications to reduce inflammation and open narrowed passages in your lungs. How to prevent yourself from getting Bronchitis

To keep from getting bronchitis, try not to be in close contact with people who have cold or flu-like symptoms. Wash your hands regularly, and don’t touch your eyes, mouth, or nose. The flu can cause bronchitis. That’s why it’s smart to get your flu shot every year. Quit smoking.[5] Studies show that people who smoke cigarettes or are exposed to a lot of secondhand smoke are much more likely to develop chronic bronchitis.[6] Because of this, it is important that you quit smoking and avoid second-hand smoke if you are concerned about getting bronchitis. Substances in the cigarettes may cause your respiratory tract to become inflamed which then makes you more prone to getting a bacterial or viral infection.
